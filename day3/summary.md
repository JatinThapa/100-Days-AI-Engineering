# 🚀 Day 3/100 – Exploring the World Behind AI

Today was a theory-focused day. No coding, no projects—just learning and understanding how modern AI systems work behind the scenes.

🧠 One of the key things I learned was that LLMs can generally be grouped into three categories:

* **Base Models** – trained to predict the next token.
* **Chat Models** – optimized for conversations and instruction-following.
* **Reasoning Models** – designed to spend more computation on complex problem-solving and multi-step reasoning.

⚠️ I also explored the strengths and limitations of frontier models. Despite being incredibly capable, they can still provide incorrect information with surprising confidence. This highlighted the importance of verification and critical thinking when working with AI systems.

🤔 Another interesting topic was how models sometimes struggle with seemingly simple questions, such as counting words in their own responses or solving unusual viral prompts. These examples showed that AI can be powerful while still having unexpected weaknesses.

🤖 The most exciting part of today was learning about **Agentic AI**. I watched demonstrations of advanced AI systems performing complex tasks by combining multiple tools and workflows. From conducting deep research over extended periods to planning restaurant recommendations based on ratings, reservations, location, and traffic conditions, it was fascinating to see how these systems operate autonomously.

✨ What amazed me most was seeing AI break down goals, gather information, use tools, and make decisions on its own. It gave me a glimpse into the future of AI-powered applications and reinforced why I'm excited to learn AI Engineering.

📚 Today's progress wasn't measured in lines of code, but in understanding the bigger picture of how modern AI systems, agents, and tools work together.
